Mahatma Gandhi Tribute Page

Mahatma Gandhi, also known as Bapu, was the leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule. Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, he led India to independence and inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

Gandhi is known for his philosophy of non-violent resistance, which he called Satyagraha, meaning "truth force". He believed in living a simple and austere life and advocated for the practice of ahimsa or non-violence.

Gandhi's legacy continues to inspire people around the world in their struggle for civil rights, freedom, and social justice.
